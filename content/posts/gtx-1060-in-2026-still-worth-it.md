The GTX 1060 is still the most-used GPU on Steam's hardware survey. That's wild for a card released in 2016. But "most popular" and "still good" aren't the same thing. Here's the real answer.

## What the 1060 Can Still Do

**At 1080p:**

- **Indie games**: Runs basically everything. Hades II, Balatro, Vampire Survivors, any 2D title — no question.
- **Older AAA (pre-2020)**: Witcher 3, Doom 2016, Fallout 4, Dark Souls 3 — all solid 60fps at medium-high settings.
- **eSports**: CS2, Valorant, Rocket League, League — 60+ fps easily at 1080p with competitive settings.

**That's the good news.** The 1060 still handles a massive chunk of the Steam library without complaint.

## Where It Falls Apart

- **Modern AAA at 1080p**: Cyberpunk, Alan Wake 2, Starfield — you're looking at 25-40fps on low, and some effects won't render correctly on the 6GB model.
- **Ray tracing**: Don't. The 1060 technically supports it but at frames-per-minute, not frames-per-second.
- **4K**: No. Stop.
- **6GB vs 3GB**: If you have the 3GB model, the gap is wider. The 3GB hits VRAM limits in games from 2023 onward and stutters badly.

## Should You Upgrade?

**Don't upgrade if:**
- You play indie, eSports, or older titles
- You're on a tight budget
- Your CPU is also old (bottleneck will follow you to a new GPU)

**Upgrade if:**
- You're trying to play new AAA releases and can't accept 30fps low
- You have a CPU from 2019+ that won't bottleneck a better GPU
- You have $200+ to spend

Used market is your friend. The RX 580 8GB at $55 is the budget king — it's 8GB VRAM, it's still supported, and it costs less than a new game.

## How to Squeeze More Life Out of It

If you're keeping the 1060:

1. **Drop to 900p.** 1600x900 gives you a 30-40% frame rate uplift vs 1080p, and the quality loss is less than you'd think on a 24" monitor.
2. **Use FSR or XeSS.** Even if the game doesn't support it natively, Lossless Scaling on Steam ($7) adds frame generation and AI upscaling to any game.
3. **Overclock the memory.** GDDR5 on the 1060 overclocks well — a 500MHz boost on the memory clock is usually stable and nets 5-8% more performance.
4. **Repaste the GPU.** If the card is 5+ years old and hitting 83°C+, repasting it with good thermal paste can drop temps 10-15°C and stop thermal throttling.

The 1060 isn't dead. But it's on life support for modern AAA. Budget where it makes sense, and don't let anyone tell you that you need a 4070 to game.
